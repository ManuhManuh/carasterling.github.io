Orienteering

This is a VR experience, brought to you by VentR-out, that is meant to "Extend the Outdoors". It features orienteer racing with a map and compass, alone or as a team. The racing locations are replicas of real life parks, using older courses created by master map makers

I was originally contracted to take care of the backend stuff, specifically PlayFab and Azure, and have moved into a lead developer position.

I have learned an incredible amount from working on this project:

  • Using Azure for server side APIs and addressables
  • Integrating SDKs: This project uses many, and sometimes they fight...
  • Multiplayer: This project is fully multiplayer, but also has to work where there is limited or no connectivity (particularly when demonstrating at events)
  • Scaling UI: Racers use maps that have a specified scale and are accurate to the terrain (render camera used for player position). This was more challenging than it might seem.
  • Working with a team of up to 10 people from four continents
  • Sticking to someone else's design: when working on solo projects or in pairs, it's really easy to fall back on "if at first you don't succeed, redefine success". When you have to stick to a design, you just have to figure out the hard stuff.
  • Adaptability: As the focus for the project changed (from game to simulation) some beloved things got dropped, and some cool new ones emerged
